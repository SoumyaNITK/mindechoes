Chapter 5: Shakuntala, Dushyanta, and the Rise of Bharata

2025-07-23

In the lineage of the lunar dynasty, after several generations, there was a powerful and noble king named Dushyanta. One day, while on a hunting expedition, he entered the hermitage of the great Sage Kanva, nestled deep in the forest. There, he met a beautiful young woman named Shakuntala, the foster daughter of the sage.

The Love of Dushyanta and Shakuntala

Shakuntala was the daughter of Sage Vishwamitra and Menaka, the celestial nymph. Menaka had left the infant Shakuntala in the care of Sage Kanva after her birth. As Shakuntala grew up in the peaceful ashram, she became wise, graceful, and radiant.

King Dushyanta was instantly captivated by her. They fell in love, and following the Gandharva marriage tradition (a marriage of mutual consent without rituals), they got married in secret. Dushyanta gave her his royal ring as a token and promise that he would send for her soon to take her to his palace.

The Curse of Sage Durvasa

Some time after Dushyanta left, the fiery Sage Durvasa visited the hermitage. Shakuntala, lost in thoughts of her husband, failed to greet him with proper respect. Enraged, Durvasa cursed her:

"He whom you are thinking about will forget you!"
