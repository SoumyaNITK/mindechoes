Chapter 4: King Bharata's Story

2025-07-22

Bharata was the son of King Dushyanta and Shakuntala, and he would become one of the greatest emperors of ancient India. His name would live on forever — so much so that India itself would come to be known as “Bharat.”

👑 Childhood and Destiny

Born in the forest hermitage of Sage Kanva, Bharata was raised in a simple, disciplined environment. Though his father Dushyanta initially forgot Shakuntala due to a curse, he later remembered and brought her and Bharata back to the palace.

Even as a boy, Bharata showed signs of greatness. Wild animals that feared everyone else would approach him calmly. He was fearless and wise beyond his years.

🌍 Rule of Righteousness

When Bharata became king, he conquered vast territories through peace, dharma, and power. He performed many Ashwamedha Yagnas (horse sacrifices) to demonstrate his sovereignty and purified his soul through these rituals.

He was a just and noble king, loved by all his people. His name became a symbol of ideal kingship.

❌ Rejection of His Sons

Though Bharata had many sons, he found none of them worthy of the throne. They were either greedy, foolish, or too soft-hearted to lead.

“A king must not follow the law of attachment,” Bharata declared.
“He must serve the law of righteousness.”

In a bold move, he rejected his own sons and chose a young boy named Bhumanyu, who had been born through the blessings of sages, to carry on his lineage.

🧬 The Bharata Dynasty

Bharata's choice set the tone for generations to come. The Bharata dynasty became one of the greatest in history, leading eventually to King Kuru, then to Shantanu, and ultimately to the Pandavas and Kauravas.

The Mahabharata is called so because it is the tale of this mighty lineage — the “descendants of Bharata.”

✨ Legacy

  • India is called Bharat in honor of this great king.
  • He proved that righteousness > bloodline.
  • His rule laid the moral foundation for all future kings in the epic.

“The worth of a man is not in his birth, but in his dharma.”
— The lesson of King Bharata
