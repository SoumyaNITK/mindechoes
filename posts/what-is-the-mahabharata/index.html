What is the Mahabharata?

2025-07-19

The Mahabharata is not just a story.
It is history, philosophy, devotion, emotion, drama, and life itself — all rolled into one.
It’s one of the greatest epics ever written in the world.

The Mahabharata was written by Sage Vyasa, also known as Vedavyasa.
It is said to have over 100,000 verses, making it over 10 times longer than the Iliad and the Odyssey combined.

But more than its length, it is the depth that makes it so timeless.

🕉️ The Core of the Epic

At the heart of the Mahabharata lies a family conflict between two groups of royal cousins:

  • The Pandavas – Yudhishthira, Bhima, Arjuna, Nakula, and Sahadeva
  • The Kauravas – led by Duryodhana and his 99 brothers

This conflict eventually leads to the Kurukshetra War — a massive war that changes everything.

🎓 What Does “Mahabharata” Mean?

  • “Maha” = Great
  • “Bharata” = Refers to the Bharata dynasty

So Mahabharata means:

“The Great Story of the Bharata Dynasty.”

But it’s much more than the story of kings.
It talks about:

  • Dharma (righteousness)
  • Karma (action)
  • Moksha (liberation)
  • And the eternal struggle between good and evil, truth and lies, love and hate

📜 Who Wrote It?

The author is Vyasa, who was himself a character in the story.
He dictated the entire epic to Lord Ganesha, who agreed to write it down only if Vyasa spoke without pause.
Vyasa cleverly added deep meanings and pauses that made even Ganesha think!

🕯️ Why Is It Still Relevant?

Because it talks about:

  • Family problems
  • Jealousy
  • Honor
  • Duty
  • Love
  • War
  • Forgiveness
  • Death

In short: everything a human can feel or face.

Whether you're a student, warrior, monk, mother, king, or ordinary person — the Mahabharata has something for you.
