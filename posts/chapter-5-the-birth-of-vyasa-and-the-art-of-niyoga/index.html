Birth of Vyasa

After King Shantanu passed away, Satyavati remained at the palace as the queen mother. But long before she married Shantanu, Satyavati had a divine encounter.

As a young girl helping her father, a fisherman, Satyavati (then known as Matsyagandha due to her fishy smell) once ferried the great sage Parashara across the river. During the journey, Parashara was enchanted by her beauty and expressed his desire for her.

Satyavati was reluctant, fearing shame and dishonor. But Parashara promised her three boons:

  • Her virginity would remain intact.
  • She would gain a divine fragrance (turning her from Matsyagandha to Yojanagandha, whose scent carried for miles).
  • Their child would be born instantly and grow up to be a wise and powerful sage.

Convinced, she agreed. Vyasa was born immediately on an island in the Yamuna River and left with Parashara to be raised in the forest. Satyavati continued her life as if nothing had happened.

The Death of Vichitravirya and a Succession Crisis

Years later, after Bhishma arranged the marriages of King Vichitravirya with Ambika and Ambalika, tragedy struck. Vichitravirya died childless from a disease, leaving the Kuru dynasty without an heir.

Satyavati was heartbroken and anxious. Without a successor, the royal lineage was doomed.

The Custom of Niyoga

In those times, a practice called Niyoga was followed: if a man died without children, his widow could bear children through a designated man, often a close relative or a sage, without violating social norms.

Satyavati, determined to save the dynasty, first approached Bhishma. But true to his terrible vow, Bhishma refused.

Left with no other choice, Satyavati called upon her secret son, Vyasa‚Äîthe great sage and seer.

Vyasa agreed, but warned that since he had just emerged from deep meditation, his appearance was wild and fearsome. Still, Satyavati insisted.

The Three Encounters

1. Ambika and Dhritarashtra

When Vyasa approached Ambika, she was terrified by his appearance and closed her eyes in fear during the act. As a result, her son Dhritarashtra was born blind.

2. Ambalika and Pandu

Next, Vyasa went to Ambalika. She turned pale with fear but kept her eyes open. Her son Pandu was born pale and sickly.

3. Ambika Again and Vidura

Unsatisfied, Satyavati requested Ambika to try again. But Ambika, frightened, sent her maid instead.
